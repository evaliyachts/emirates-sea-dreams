## Doorway-page safeguards

Every surviving rental, for-rent, hire, charter, booking and luxury page must declare one distinct primary intent and satisfy a different visitor decision. Build tests must reject duplicate normalized:

- primary intent;
- title;
- meta description;
- H1;
- introduction;
- FAQ question set;
- section-heading set.

The test should also flag high textual similarity for human review. A page fails if its only difference is a synonym in the slug/title. Grammar variants remain secondary terms on the strongest owner.

## Internal-link anchors

- Root → catalogue: “compare verified yachts and hourly prices.”
- Root/catalogue → booking: “send your date, duration and guest count.”
- Commercial owner → event hub: “plan a private yacht celebration.”
- Event pages → yachts: use yacht names or decision-based anchors, not repeated “yacht rental Dubai.”
- Avoid sitewide exact-match anchors for every keyword variant.
